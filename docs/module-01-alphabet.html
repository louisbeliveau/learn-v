Vietnamese for Beginners

Module 1: The Alphabet

Bảng chữ cái tiếng Việt  ·  Learning the 29 letters & 6 tones

Introduction

Vietnamese uses the Latin alphabet (chữ Quốc ngữ), so you will recognise many letters. However, Vietnamese adds diacritical marks (dấu) that change both a letter's sound and its tone — making two separate systems to learn: the alphabet (29 letters) and the tones (6 tones).

Key fact Vietnamese has 29 letters. The letters F, J, W and Z exist in borrowed words but are not part of the official alphabet. Every syllable in Vietnamese carries exactly one of six tones, marked directly on the vowel.

Part 2 — The 6 Tones

Tones are not optional — they change the meaning of a word completely. They are marked with small symbols placed above or below the main vowel of a syllable.

# Name Mark Description Example Meaning
1Ngang (flat)— Mid level, steady. No mark written. maghost
2Huyền (falling)à Low falling, slightly breathy. màbut / whereas
3Sắc (rising)á High rising, like a surprised question. mácheek / mum (South)
4Hỏi (dipping)ả Mid dipping then rising, like a questioning "really?" mảtomb / grave
5Ngã (broken rising)ã Rising with a glottal break/creak in the middle. mãhorse (Sino-Viet.) / code
6Nặng (heavy falling)ạ Low falling, short and heavy, ends abruptly. mạrice seedling
Memory trick: the "ma" family All six tones can be heard on the syllable ma: ma · mà · má · mả · mã · mạ — ghost, but, cheek, tomb, code, seedling. Practise saying these six words in order until the pitch shapes feel natural.
